# ECAT-ICAT Glossary

## Purpose

This glossary defines the release-candidate vocabulary for ECAT/ICAT testing. These terms are formal governance terms, not clinical, diagnostic, surveillance, or certification terms.

## Terms

### Admissibility

The condition under which a transition, claim, artifact, relationship, boundary, or state is allowed to carry standing under declared governance conditions.

### BCAT

Boundary Constraint Analysis. BCAT evaluates whether a boundary remains recoverable, scoped, non-inverting, and suitable for later governance determination.

### Boundary relevance

The declared reason an ECAT or ICAT profile matters to a later boundary review. Boundary relevance does not grant authority by itself.

### Coherence status

The declared ECAT status for whether the profile can support a stable governance interpretation. RC1 values are `coherent`, `partial`, `incoherent`, and `unknown`.

### Continuity

The ability to reconstruct a state, identity, claim, artifact, relationship, or proof path across a boundary.

### ECAT

Emotional / Experiential Constraint Analysis. ECAT models the declared internal experience layer before a governance boundary hardens into authority.

ECAT is not emotional surveillance and is not psychological diagnosis.

### GCAT

Governance Constraint Analysis. GCAT evaluates governance standing, policy, delegation, authority, admissibility, and fail-closed execution decisions.

### ICAT

Interpersonal / Intuitive Constraint Analysis. ICAT models declared inter-entity context before a boundary, trust claim, witness claim, delegation claim, or authority claim is treated as admissible.

ICAT is not relationship certification and is not a blanket authority grant.

### Recoverability status

The declared status for whether the profile can be revisited, paused, reconstructed, or bounded without forcing execution. RC1 values are `recoverable`, `conditional`, `unrecoverable`, and `unknown`.

### Standing

The current admissible status of a transition, claim, state, relationship, or boundary at the moment it is evaluated.

### Triad governance

The three-part governance frame separating transition governance, admissibility governance, and continuity governance.

### %Existence

A governed existence expression describing how strongly an entity, relationship, claim, transition, or state may be treated as existing under the relevant governance frame.

In ECAT/ICAT terms, `%Existence` is not mere observation. It depends on alignment across experience, relationship context, boundary recoverability, and governance admissibility.
